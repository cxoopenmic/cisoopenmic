Overview

CISO Open Mic brings security leaders together for candid, peer-led discussion. No slideware, no sales pitches — just real conversations about what’s working (and what isn’t).

Before the Event

1) Register

Sign up on Lu.ma. If a waitlist forms, we’ll release additional spots in batches.

2) Read the Brief

We share a short email with the evening’s prompts and venue details 48–72 hours before.

3) Come as You Are

Business casual works. Bring questions, lessons learned, and curiosity.

During the Event

Check-in & Networking

Arrive, grab a drink, meet peers. We start the Open Mic shortly after doors open.

Open Mic

Host intro, 2–3 prompts, then the room leads. Short, focused shares — no decks, no pitches.

Close & Mingle

We wrap on time and leave room for follow-ups and intros.

After the Event

  • Takeaways: Short recap with anonymized highlights (no attribution).
  • Connections: Opt-in attendees may receive polite follow-ups or intros.
  • Next Cities: Watch the calendar for new dates.

House Rules

  • No slideware, no pitches. Community conversation first.
  • Be concise. Share the signal; keep stories tight.
  • Respect time. Give others the mic.
  • Be kind. Disagree without being disagreeable.

Accessibility: If you have accessibility needs or dietary restrictions, reply to your confirmation email — we’ll accommodate.

Privacy & Chatham House Rule

We follow the spirit of the Chatham House Rule: use the information received, but do not reveal the identity or affiliation of speakers. No recording or live-posting.

FAQs

Who can attend?
Security leaders and adjacent exec/manager roles.

Are vendors allowed?
Yes — as community members or sponsors. “No pitch” rule applies.

Is there a cost?
Usually complimentary thanks to sponsors; some cities may have nominal tickets.

Will there be food?
Yes — light dinner and beverages.

Can I suggest a topic?
Absolutely. Use the contact link below.

What if I need to cancel?
Please release your ticket via Lu.ma for waitlisted guests.
